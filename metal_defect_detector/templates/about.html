About Metal Defect Detector

This application uses deep learning models to detect and classify various types of metal surface defects, providing Grad-CAM visualizations to pinpoint defect locations.

Data Balancing

The original dataset contained an imbalanced distribution of defect classes, leading to biased model predictions. We implemented data augmentation strategies to create a balanced dataset with 50 images per class.

Augmentation techniques used:
  • Spatial transformations (rotation, flipping, scaling)
  • Color and contrast adjustments
  • Noise and blur variations
  • Texture enhancements for defect visualization
  • Geometric distortions to simulate real-world conditions

The balanced dataset significantly improves model performance and reduces bias toward overrepresented classes.

Enhanced Visualization

We improved the Grad-CAM visualization to provide clearer indications of detected defect regions. The improved visualization includes:

  • Higher resolution output (200 DPI)
  • Enhanced contrast for better visibility
  • Proper RGB color handling for consistent visualization
  • Histogram equalization to highlight subtle features
  • Increased heatmap weight for more prominent defect highlighting

These improvements make it easier to locate and understand detected defects in metal surfaces.

Models Used

The system employs multiple state-of-the-art deep learning architectures to ensure robust defect detection:

  • ResNet50: Deep residual network with 50 layers, excellent at learning complex features without degradation
  • EfficientNet: Optimized CNN architecture with balanced depth, width, and resolution for efficient learning
  • DenseNet: Densely connected network where each layer receives inputs from all previous layers, capturing fine details
  • MobileNet: Lightweight architecture designed for mobile devices, providing fast inference times

The models are combined using ensemble techniques to improve the overall accuracy and robustness of defect classification.

Defect Categories

The system can identify the following categories of metal surface defects:

  • Crazing: Network of fine cracks on the metal surface
  • Cracks: Linear breaks or fractures in the metal
  • Inclusions: Foreign particles embedded in the metal matrix
  • Scratches: Linear marks or grooves on the surface
  • Indentation: Localized depression or dent in the surface
  • Oil Spots: Discolored areas due to oil contamination
  • Rolling: Surface defects from rolling process
  • Rusting: Oxidation on the metal surface
  • Welding Line: Defects along welding seams
